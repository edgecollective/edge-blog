    <div class="section">
      <b>ground</b> is a curated set of designs for sensor and communications networks that is focused on network freedom, collaboration, and resilience.
    </div>
    <div class="section">
      The <b>ground</b> project is intended to curate and develop FOSS (free-and-open-source), interoperable software and hardware that enable farmers and land & water resource managers to gain greater control over their sensor data.
    </div>
    <div class="section">
      <b>ground</b> designs use a <b>'local-first'</b> networking approach:  sensor and communications data are stored locally on-site first, and are always locally available -- even if internet is currently not. Data can then be <b>selectively shared</b> at the user's discretion with others through a variety of methods and formats.
    </div>
     <div class="section">
      <b>ground</b> is an alternative to most commercially-available networked environmental monitoring systems, which usually store data primarily on the cloud, and don't always allow users to store and access their data locally 'offline'.
    </div>
